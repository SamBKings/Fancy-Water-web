# Product

## Register

brand

## Users

Mixed audience in Mexico/LatAm, browsing in Spanish, mostly on mobile (arriving via Meta/Google ads and WhatsApp):

- **Aesthetic-medicine professionals** — doctors, cosmiatras, clinic owners evaluating whether Fancy Water is a legitimate, premium source for VOL:TENA botulinum toxins, dermal fillers, PLLA, lipoenzymes, and skin boosters.
- **End consumers** — individuals buying directly for personal use, less technical, more sensitive to trust signals and ease of checkout.

Both groups share one job: verify this is a real, authorized distributor, then buy with confidence.

## Product Purpose

Fancy Water is the official VOL:TENA distributor for the Americas. The site is a single-page storefront: product catalog with cart and Stripe checkout, shipping quotes, technical data sheets (fichas), contact/chat channels, and conversion tracking (Meta Pixel, Google Ads).

**Success = completed Stripe purchases.** The site is a conversion machine for direct online sales; inquiry channels (WhatsApp, chat, contact form) support that goal but are secondary.

## Brand Personality

**Clinical luxury.** Premium, trustworthy, editorial — a high-end medical journal meets a fashion house. Paper-and-ink palette, serif display headlines (Playfair Display), restrained navy accent, generous whitespace. The restraint itself is the trust signal: it says "official distributor," not "discount reseller."

## Anti-references

- **Gray-market pharma shops** — cluttered product grids, price badges everywhere, fake urgency timers, sketchy trust seals. Fancy Water's whole value is legitimacy; anything that pattern-matches to gray-market kills it.
- **Generic beauty e-commerce** — pink/pastel cosmetics-store aesthetics aimed at retail skincare shoppers. This is medical-grade product, not a beauty haul.
- **Template landing pages** — interchangeable SaaS-style sections, stock icons, no brand identity.

## Design Principles

1. **Legitimacy through restraint** — every element should read "official distributor." Calm typography, real product photography, and precise details beat badges and urgency tactics.
2. **Frictionless path to checkout** — success is a completed Stripe purchase. Every section should shorten the distance from interest to cart to payment, especially on mobile.
3. **Serve two readers at once** — professionals need technical depth (fichas, concentrations, origin); consumers need clarity and reassurance. Layer information so neither audience is alienated.
4. **Mobile is the primary stage** — traffic arrives from ads and WhatsApp on phones. Design and test mobile-first; desktop is the enhancement.
5. **Editorial, not promotional** — speak with the confidence of an authority, not the volume of a discounter.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

WCAG AA (4.5:1 body-text contrast, keyboard navigable, `prefers-reduced-motion` alternatives) with **extra emphasis on mobile**: comfortable touch targets (≥44px), thumb-reachable primary actions, and layouts tested on small screens, since most traffic is mobile.
