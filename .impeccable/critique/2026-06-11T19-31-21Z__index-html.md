## Anti-Patterns Verdict

**LLM assessment:** Passes the first glance — real product photography, coherent paper-and-ink system, committed serif voice. But two pieces of AI grammar survive: a tiny uppercase tracked eyebrow above all seven sections (Catálogo, Pedidos, Comunidad, Testimonios, Por qué Fancy Water, Producto Estrella, Quiénes Somos), and the benefits section is the identical icon+heading+text card grid with a visibly longer fifth card. Playfair+Inter is on the saturated-font list, but it is committed brand identity here — identity preservation wins; not flagged for replacement.

**Deterministic scan (CLI, 13 findings):** 9× overused-font (Inter, partial false positive given committed identity), 1× bounce-easing (payment toast `cubic-bezier(.34,1.56,.64,1)` — violates the no-bounce rule and the system's own restrained-motion doctrine), 2× broken-image (modal `<img src="">` placeholders at lines 2377/2434 — populated by JS, but ship as empty src), 1× em-dash density in copy.

**Browser scan (injected detector):** confirmed the kickers (7), plus findings the static scan can't see:
- **low-contrast 2.0:1** — Ash #B7B4AC on Snow for `.cart-item-sub` (price per unit IN THE CART), `.checkout-note` (payment reassurance line), `.contact-lbl`, `.test-role`, `.section-label`, price-note and the "Producto sobre pedido" disclaimer. Several of these are real content, not decorative labels.
- **low-contrast 4.2:1** — Grafito #7A7975 on Snow for testimonial body text, about/contact paragraphs. The system's designated body color misses WCAG AA (4.5:1) on its own surfaces.
- **tiny-text** — 11px running text in checkout note.
- **skipped-heading** — h2 → h4 jump into footer columns.

**Visual overlays:** detector injection succeeded in the preview tab; console findings above. Note: this preview renderer ghosts `backdrop-filter` layers in screenshots (cart/checkout modals look washed out in captures but are correctly stacked and interactive — verified via elementFromPoint).

## Overall Impression

The Clinical Atelier voice is real: this does not look like a template, the photography is decisive, and the catalog/ficha system serves professionals unusually well. The gap is between brand surface and conversion machine — the page sells legitimacy, then the checkout path leaks: hidden catalog majority, volatile cart, an unexplained disabled pay button, a chat bubble sitting on the pay CTA, and body text that misses the site's own AA commitment. Fixing the funnel mechanics is worth more right now than any visual change.

## What's Working

1. **Decisive, real product imagery** — hero slideshow, navy showcase, catalog and fichas all use real product photography with consistent art direction; the imagery mandate most sites in this category fail is genuinely met.
2. **Fichas técnicas** — fabricante, composición, presentación, protocolos, indicaciones per product. Clinician-grade contextual help that directly serves the B2B reader from PRODUCT.md.
3. **Mobile performance doctrine** — Three.js skipped under 960px, GPU effects disabled under 640px, IntersectionObservers pause offscreen work. Real engineering for the primary stage.

## Priority Issues

**[P1] The conversion path is volatile and under-explained**
- Why: cart is a JS array — a reload, an app switch on mobile, or backing out of Stripe wipes it. "Pagar Ahora" is disabled until a shipping rate is chosen but nothing says so. Failure path is a raw browser `alert()`. This is the success metric leaking.
- Fix: persist cart to localStorage (restore on load); enable "Pagar Ahora" with inline hint "Cotiza tu envío para continuar" replacing the silent disable; swap alert() for the existing inline error pattern; close drawer when checkout opens; wire Esc to cart/checkout.
- Suggested command: /impeccable harden

**[P1] Landing state hides 79% of the catalog**
- Why: `applyFilter('toxinas')` boots the grid with 4 of 19 products and there is no "Todos" chip; a consumer who doesn't know category names never sees the rest.
- Fix: add a "Todos" chip as default-active (or boot unfiltered with Toxinas first in source order).
- Suggested command: /impeccable polish

**[P1] Body text misses the site's own WCAG AA bar**
- Why: Grafito (#7A7975) = 4.2:1 on Snow — testimonials, descriptions, about copy all fail AA; Ash (#B7B4AC) = 2.0:1 is used for real content (cart unit price, checkout reassurance, disclaimers). PRODUCT.md commits to AA.
- Fix: darken Grafito one step (≈#6B6A66 hits 4.6:1 on Snow); promote cart-item-sub/checkout-note/disclaimers from Ash to (new) Grafito; keep Ash strictly for ≤12px uppercase labels. Update DESIGN.md tokens to match.
- Suggested command: /impeccable polish

**[P1] Chat FAB sits on the checkout CTA**
- Why: chat-fab (fixed, bottom 28/right 28, z-500) overlaps the right end of "Proceder al Pago" in the 400px cart drawer (z-201) on desktop — a competing tap target at the decision moment.
- Fix: hide or shift the chat FAB while cart drawer or checkout modal is open (one class toggle in toggleCart/openCheckout).
- Suggested command: /impeccable polish

**[P2] AI-grammar cadence on a page that otherwise has voice**
- Why: 7 identical ash eyebrows + the icon-card benefits grid are the two patterns that read "generated"; the bounce toast easing breaks the motion doctrine; red cart badge breaks the One Voice rule.
- Fix: keep at most one named kicker as a brand system (or drop them — the Playfair italic already carries cadence); rework benefits as a single editorial paragraph block or asymmetric list; ease-out the toast; navy badge.
- Suggested command: /impeccable typeset (cadence) + /impeccable quieter (benefits grid)

**[P2] Trust-surface loose ends**
- Why: "Agregar a la Lista" is leftover from the inquiry era; dead Aviso de Privacidad/Términos links and placeholder social icons undermine the official-distributor claim at the exact trust-check moment; checkout modal has no max-height/scroll (overflow risk on short phones); zero prefers-reduced-motion coverage.
- Fix: unify CTA copy on "Agregar al Carrito"; real legal pages or remove links; `max-height:90dvh; overflow:auto` on the modal; one reduced-motion block disabling marquee/float/orbit/reveal transitions.
- Suggested command: /impeccable harden

## Persona Red Flags

**Casey (distracted mobile buyer):** cart dies on app-switch (in-memory); browsing 19 products one 78vw card at a time; must type a CP mid-checkout; FAB stack crowds the thumb zone above the drawer footer. Will abandon between cart and Stripe.

**Jordan (consumer first-timer):** lands on 4 products and may believe that's the catalog; "Tarifa Minorista" and "IVA no incluido" unexplained; taps Términos y Condiciones at the trust moment and gets `#`. Will message WhatsApp instead of checking out — or leave.

**Riley (stress tester):** checkout modal can overflow short viewports with no scroll; newsletter API failure silently resets the button as if nothing happened; double-tapping add silently increments qty (drawer opens, acceptable); empty-src modal images flash broken on slow loads.

**Dra. Valeria (project persona — repeat B2B buyer, from CLAUDE.md design context):** no order history, no reorder, cart doesn't survive a session; volume buying routed to WhatsApp with no price tiers visible. The highest-LTV user has the least-served path.
